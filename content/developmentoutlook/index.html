Development Organizations: Today’s Challenges and Opportunities

The era of modern fundraising and development in the United States is commonly thought to have started in the early 1900s, with the efforts of Charles Ward and Franklin Pierce to raise money for the YMCA. Their 1905 campaign is reported to have been the first to use techniques such as corporate-sponsored advertising and a fixed, short-duration campaign clock.

Today, charitable donations are at an all-time high, with an estimated $410 billion in donations by American individuals, estates, foundations and corporation in 2017, according to a recent report. To support a wide range of medical, humanitarian, community, conservation, education, and other endeavors, non-profit leaders are using sophisticated engagement and nurturing models, complex donor management and relationship systems, highly iterative social campaigns, and teams of highly skilled professionals whose talents span strategy, prospect research, event planning, and more.
 

Challenges

However, this means that the pressure on nonprofit development organizations to excel is higher than ever before. Challenges facing these organizations include:

  • Competition for Time and Attention from Other Charitable Organizations: The ease of online and social media communication has exponentially increased the number and variety of fundraising solicitations that donors are exposed to. Anyone can set up a “birthday fundraiser” on Facebook, GoFundMe has raised billions of dollars through millions of campaigns, and email marketing automation generates nearly endless content for our inboxes.
  • Meeting Constituent Expectations of High-Touch Service: Both in the for-profit and non-profit sectors, high-touch customer service is increasingly demanded and expected, and organizations are acting accordingly, with a majority utilizing a high-touch customer service strategy. According to recent studies, over 80% of organizational leaders see an increasing importance of customer service and success compared to just three years ago. For development organizations, this can mean an increase in the size of skilled workforces as well as investments in technology to more efficiently and effectively engage and nurture constituents, such as market automation tools to design and execute complex, targeted, and multi-step marketing campaigns.
  • Disparate Systems: Nonprofit organizations need to choose the right systems and combinations of systems, from a vast array of software, and integrate them successfully on an ongoing basis. Non-profits have similar, if not greater requirements, than for-profit marketing organizations, across advertising, content, user experience, social media, data, and more.
  • Adapting to the Fast Change of Technology: Technology innovation simultaneously creates opportunity and disruption, and the cost is in both time and money. According to a 2017 PwC survey, a majority of companies are making “substantial investments” in the Internet of Things and Artificial Intelligence, but a minority have sufficient skills in those areas. The gap is even more pronounced in other focus areas, such as predictive analytics, “hyper” targeting and segmentation, chat bots, and more.

 

Opportunities

Despite the challenges, there are significant opportunities which can lead to successful results in driving an organization’s mission. The following are just a few key forces and innovations that are shaping the development landscape:

  1. New Channels of Engagement: Mobile and social means of engagement have matured, allowing for organizations to connect more quickly and more directly with current and potential donors. Live video, user-generated content, and influencer marketing help communicate more personalized storytelling, deepening roots and connections across multiple physical and virtual communities. It is important to note that these new forms of engagement and technology are not replacements for existing channels, but rather serve to augment: people spend more and more time on their phones, but also highly value human connection and a personal touch. At the same time, Big Data behind social media advertising enables organizations to maximize the ROI of their outreach efforts.
  2. Cloud-Based Solutions: Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) solutions have proliferated across the nonprofit sector, targeting both broad and niche needs. Understanding the best combination of products and services and how to integrate them can be difficult. Effective solutions require the making the right decisions and tradeoffs around features, cost, scalability, and risk.
  3. Global Opportunities: As the standard of living and prosperity of countries around the world increases, so do the opportunities for nonprofits to fulfill their missions and to develop new sources of funding and support. University endowments and capital projects have been buoyed by international donors, and organizations can simultaneously be global and hyper-local, nurturing leads and executing projects both near and far. Development organizations can bring seamless, high-touch service, with technology acting as infrastructure and an enabler.

 

Planning for Success

Nonprofit leaders are facing a greater number of challenges than ever before, and yet are poised to take advantage of more opportunities than ever. Increased giving, new forms of engagement, global audiences, and tailor-made enabling technologies are changing the landscape of fundraising and development. Ultimately careful strategy, planning, and implementation will determine success.
